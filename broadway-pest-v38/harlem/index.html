Harlem Pest Landscape

Why Harlem Businesses
Face Distinct Challenges

Harlem's combination of aging building stock, a booming restaurant scene, and rapid residential conversion creates pest pressure that generic extermination services aren't equipped to handle.

1800s
Aging Building Infrastructure
Many of Harlem's brownstones and commercial buildings date to the late 1800s. Aging foundations, original plumbing chases, and decades of patchwork construction create extensive entry points and harborage that require IPM expertise — not just spray-and-pray treatments.
125th St
Dense Restaurant Corridor
The 125th Street commercial strip and Frederick Douglass Blvd restaurant row are among NYC's fastest-growing food corridors. High density of food businesses means pest pressure migrates between properties. A neighbor's infestation becomes your DOH problem within weeks.
HPD
HPD Violation Exposure
Harlem's residential buildings — especially rent-stabilized and NYCHA-adjacent properties — face significant HPD pest violation pressure. Property managers without documented IPM programs are routinely cited. Our service logs are built to satisfy HPD inspectors on first review.
